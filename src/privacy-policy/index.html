No Attorney-Client Relationship

The information on this website is for general informational purposes only and does not constitute legal advice. Viewing this website or contacting us does not create an attorney-client relationship.

Document Upload Disclaimer: Any upload of documents through this website does not create an attorney-client relationship between the firm and the person uploading such documents. A separate representation agreement must be executed between the parties to establish an attorney-client relationship.

Not Legal Advice

You should not act or refrain from acting on the basis of any content included on this website without seeking appropriate legal or other professional advice.

Information We Collect

We collect information that you provide directly to us when you interact with our website or services. This includes, but is not limited to:

  • Contact Information: Name, email address, postal address, telephone number, and other contact details you provide when filling out our contact form, requesting a consultation, or communicating with us.
  • Case Information: Details about your legal matter, including but not limited to facts, circumstances, documents, and other information you provide in connection with seeking legal representation or consultation.
  • Documentation: Files, documents, images, and other materials you upload through our website or transmit to us electronically.
  • Communication Records: Correspondence, emails, messages, and other communications between you and our firm.
  • Technical Information: When you visit our website, we may automatically collect certain technical information, including your IP address, browser type, device information, operating system, pages viewed, time spent on pages, and referring website addresses.
  • Cookies and Tracking Technologies: We may use cookies, web beacons, and similar tracking technologies to enhance your experience, analyze usage patterns, and improve our website functionality.

How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • Legal Services: To provide legal representation, consultation, advice, and related services in accordance with attorney-client relationships and applicable professional rules.
  • Communication: To respond to your inquiries, provide information about our services, schedule consultations, and communicate regarding your legal matters.
  • Case Management: To evaluate potential legal matters, conduct conflict checks, manage client relationships, and maintain case files in accordance with our professional obligations.
  • Legal Compliance: To comply with applicable laws, regulations, court orders, and professional rules of conduct, including but not limited to requirements for maintaining client records and responding to legal process.
  • Website Improvement: To analyze website usage, improve user experience, enhance website functionality, and optimize our online presence.
  • Business Operations: To manage our practice, process payments, maintain records, and conduct internal business operations.
  • Marketing: With your consent, to send you newsletters, updates, and information about legal developments that may be of interest, subject to your ability to opt-out at any time.

Information Sharing and Disclosure

We respect the confidentiality of your information and do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for marketing purposes. We may share your information in the following circumstances:

  • With Your Consent: We may share information when you have given us explicit permission to do so.
  • Legal Services: In the course of providing legal representation, we may share information with co-counsel, experts, consultants, and other professionals necessary to effectively represent your interests, all subject to confidentiality obligations.
  • Service Providers: We may share information with trusted third-party service providers who assist us in operating our business, such as technology providers, payment processors, and administrative services, all of whom are contractually obligated to maintain confidentiality and use information only for specified purposes.
  • Legal Requirements: We may disclose information when required by law, court order, subpoena, or other legal process, or when necessary to protect our rights, property, or safety, or that of our clients or others.
  • Professional Obligations: We may disclose information as required by applicable rules of professional conduct, including but not limited to reporting obligations and responses to disciplinary inquiries.
  • Business Transfers: In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction, subject to the same privacy protections.

Data Security

We implement commercially reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed to protect your personal information against unauthorized access, use, disclosure, alteration, or destruction. These measures include:

  • Encryption of sensitive data in transit and at rest
  • Secure server infrastructure and access controls
  • Regular security assessments and updates
  • Employee training on data protection and confidentiality
  • Physical security measures for our offices and facilities
  • Secure document storage and retention practices

However, no method of transmission over the Internet or electronic storage is completely secure. While we strive to protect your information, we cannot guarantee absolute security. You use our website and services at your own risk, and you are responsible for taking appropriate precautions when transmitting information to us.

Data Retention

We retain your personal information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this Privacy Policy, unless a longer retention period is required or permitted by law. Factors that determine retention periods include:

  • The nature and purpose of the information collected
  • Legal and professional obligations, including rules requiring retention of client files
  • The existence of ongoing legal matters or potential claims
  • Statutory limitations periods and legal hold requirements

When information is no longer needed, we will securely delete or destroy it in accordance with our document retention policies and applicable legal requirements.

Your Rights and Choices

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have certain rights regarding your personal information, including:

  • Access: The right to request access to and copies of your personal information we hold
  • Correction: The right to request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information
  • Deletion: The right to request deletion of your personal information, subject to legal and professional obligations that may require retention
  • Objection: The right to object to certain processing activities
  • Restriction: The right to request restriction of processing in certain circumstances
  • Portability: The right to receive your information in a structured, commonly used format
  • Opt-Out: The right to opt-out of marketing communications at any time

To exercise these rights, please contact us using the information provided below. We will respond to your request in accordance with applicable law. Please note that certain information may be exempt from such requests due to legal, professional, or other obligations, including attorney-client privilege and work product protections.
